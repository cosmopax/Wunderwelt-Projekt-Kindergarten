# PHASE 2: TACTICAL ACTION MODULES
*Wunderwelt: Projekt Kindergarten - Urban Autarky Blueprint*

## Executive Summary
To bridge the gap between high-level theory ("Urban Autarky") and daily reality, we are launching three new **Action Modules**. These are low-tech, high-impact interventions designed to be deployable by a 150-person node within 30 days using locally sourced materials.

## üçÑ Module Beta: Lignin-Fungal Digesters
**Objective:** Hyper-Local Waste-to-Resource Cycling.
**The Problem:** "Waste" is a design flaw. Nutrient-rich biomass (cardboard, woody trim, coffee) is currently trucked away.
**The Solution:** Distributed myco-cycling stations. Low-tech anaerobic and aerobic fungal digestors that turn cellulose into high-grade soil for the vertical farms.
**Key Components:**
*   **Hardware:** Stacked HDPE buckets or localized wooden pallet bins.
*   **Biology:** Oyster Mushroom (*Pleurotus ostreatus*) spawn for aggressive decomposition.
*   **Output:** Protein (edible mushrooms) + Soil Amendment (spent substrate).
**Impact:**
*   Reduces waste volume by ~60%.
*   Generates high-quality topsoil (crucial for autarky).
*   Educational observation of life cycles.

**Objective:** Analog Security & Social Cohesion.
**The Problem:** Reliance on electronic surveillance (cameras) breeds distrust and fragility.
**The Solution:** "Soft-Eyes" design interventions. Retrofitting sightlines to maximize **Natural Surveillance** (Defensible Space Theory). We replace cameras with engaged neighbors.
**Key Components:**
*   **Design:** Removal of sight-blocking hedges/fences (replaced with transparent boundaries).
*   **Infrastructure:** "Social Benches" positioned at intersection nodes to encourage loitering-with-intent.
*   **Lighting:** Warm-spectrum, motion-triggered analog lighting that highlights faces, not just spaces.
**Impact:**
*   Increases actual safety via "Eyes on the Street."
*   Strengthens social bonds (the Dunbar Node).
*   Zero digital privacy intrusion.

Lignin-Fungal Digesters

Distributed Soil Generation & Protein Synthesis

The Challenge: The Waste Illusion

We treat biomass—cardboard, coffee grounds, woody trim—as "waste" to be trucked away at great carbon cost. This is a design flaw. In a kindergarten, nothing is wasted; everything is a building block.

The Intervention

Transform trash into Table & Terra.

We deploy distributed myco-cycling stations. These low-tech biological reactors use aggressive fungal strains to break down tough carbon (lignin) that compost piles can't handle, outputting high-grade soil for the vertical farms.

Technical Specifications

Agent Pleurotus ostreatus (Oyster Mushroom)
Input Stream Cardboard, Coffee, Sawdust, Cotton
Cycle Time 4-6 Weeks
Output Ratio 1kg Substrate = 400g Soil
⚠️ SAFETY WARNING: Mushrooms are hyper-accumulators. They will absorb heavy metals and toxins from inks, treated wood, or plastics in the substrate. Do not consume mushrooms grown on urban waste streams. Use fruit bodies for compost/soil building only.

The Protocol

  1. Source the Substrate: Collect the neighborhood's carbon: Amazon boxes (shredded), coffee chaff from the local roaster, and hardwood chips from tree trimming.
  2. Pasteurization (Low-Tech): Soak the substrate in lime water (pH 12) for 12 hours. This kills competitor molds without needing energy-intensive heat.
  3. Inoculation: Mix the drained substrate with grain spawn in stacked HDPE buckets with drilled air holes.
  4. Fruiting & Harvest: Keep in a humid, shady spot. Harvest mushrooms after ~4 weeks. Once exhausted, the remaining block is premium "spent mushroom substrate"—a super-fertilizer for the vertical farms.

Why It Matters

Soil is the foundation of autarky. By keeping biomass local, we close the nutrient loop. We turn a disposal cost into a production asset, proving that abundance is simply a matter of connecting the right systems.

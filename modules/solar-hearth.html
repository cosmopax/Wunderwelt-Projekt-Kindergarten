Helio-Thermal Hearth

Zero-Carbon Community Kitchens Powered by Concentrated Light

The Challenge: The Lonely Stove

Cooking is the third largest energy consumer in domestic life. Currently, we cook alone, using gas or resistive electricity. This atomization destroys community and wastes energy. We need a return to the communal fire, but without the smoke.

The Intervention

Cooking with Pure Photons.

We construct high-efficiency solar kitchens using Scheffler reflectors. These are not toys; they are industrial-grade thermal engines capable of baking, boiling, and frying for 50 people using nothing but focused sunlight.

The Protocol

  1. The Geometry: Construct a 2.7m¬≤ parabolic dish from polished aluminum sheets or mylar-coated steel. The curve must be precise to focus light onto a single "fire spot."
  2. The Mechanical Clock: The dish sits on a polar axis mount. A simple gravity-driven clockwork mechanism rotates the dish at 15 degrees per hour, tracking the sun automatically.
  3. The Hearth: The focal point enters the kitchen through a window or wall, striking the bottom of a cast-iron pot or griddle. The cook stays inside, in the shade; the heat is delivered remotely.
  4. Thermal Battery: For evening cooking, the focus hits a sand/iron thermal battery, storing heat for release up to 4 hours after sunset.

Why It Matters

This creates a gravitational center for the neighborhood. When the sun is high, the "Hearth" is open. It synchronizes the community's metabolism with the solar cycle and eliminates the carbon footprint of our most fundamental act: feeding each other.

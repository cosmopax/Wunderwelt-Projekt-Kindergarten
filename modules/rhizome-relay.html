The Rhizome Relay

A Solar-Powered Nervous System for the Community

The Challenge: Digital Fragility

Our communication relies on massive, centralized ISP infrastructure. In a crisis (power outage, censorship, natural disaster), the node goes dark. We need a communication layer that is as resilient as mycelium‚Äîone that works when the lights go out.

The Intervention

Civilization in a Backpack.

We deploy an off-grid, solar-powered mesh network using LoRa (Long Range) radio frequencies. This creates a "Rhizome"‚Äîa decentralized text and map network that allows the 150-person node to coordinate, trade, and assist each other without the internet.

Technical Specifications

Protocol Meshtastic (LoRa 868/915 MHz)
Range 1-5km Urban / 20km LoS
Power Draw < 1 Watt (Solar Sustainable)
Autarky Cost ~‚Ç¨40 per Node

The Protocol

  1. The Neuron: Flash an ESP32 LoRa board (like a LILYGO or Heltec) with the open-source Meshtastic firmware. This is your radio.
  2. The Power Source: Connect a simple 3000mAh 18650 battery and a 5W solar panel. House it in a waterproof PVC casing. This makes the node immortal.
  3. The High Ground: Place "Router Nodes" on the highest rooftops in the neighborhood. They act as repeaters, bouncing signals over obstacles.
  4. The Interface: Users connect via Bluetooth on their existing smartphones. The phone becomes just a screen; the network is in the air.

Why It Matters

Information is the glue of society. By owning our physical layer of communication, we ensure that the Node can govern itself under any conditions. It enables local "Craigslists," emergency alerts, and governance voting without reliance on corporate servers.

Module Alpha

Bio-Thermal Facade Armor

Passive Climate Control via Living Architectural Skins

The Challenge: The Heat Island

Concrete and glass are thermal sponges. In summer, they absorb solar radiation, creating urban heat islands that demand massive, fragility-inducing air conditioning loads. We fight the sun with electricity, a battle we are losing.

The Intervention

Don't block the sun. Metabolize it.

We deploy a "Third Skin" for our buildings: cable-guided vertical ecosystems using rapid-growth biomass. This isn't just decoration; it is functional thermal armor.

Technical Specifications

Mechanism Deciduous Evapotranspiration
Primary Biology Humulus lupulus (Hops) or Wisteria
Thermal Delta -5°C Indoor / -15°C Surface
Autarky Cost ~€200 per 10m² segment

The Protocol

  1. Anchor the Geometry: Install heavy-duty steel eyelets or reclaimed climbing anchors at the roofline and the base foundation.
  2. String the Harp: Run tensioned steel cables or marine-grade hemp rope vertically, spaced 40cm apart. This is the skeleton.
  3. The Biological Engine: Plant deciduous climbers at the base. We prefer Hops for their extreme growth rate (up to 30cm/day) and harvestable yield, or Beans for caloric output.
  4. Hydrological Link: Connect the base planters to rooftop rain barrel overflow. Gravity does the work.

Why It Matters

This is intelligent shading. In summer, the dense leaf canopy blocks 90% of solar gain. In winter, the leaves drop, allowing the sun to passively heat the thermal mass of the wall. It is a biological thermostat that requires zero electricity.

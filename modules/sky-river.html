Sky-River Catchment

Decentralized Hydrological Sovereignty for the 150-Node

The Challenge: The Fragile Pipe

Modern cities treat water as a linear commodity: pumped from afar, used once, and flushed away. This system is brittle. A single break in the municipal chain leaves the node dry. To be autonomous, we must close the loop.

The Intervention

Catch the Sky. Filter the Earth.

We decouple from the grid by turning every surface into a catchment basin. Using Coanda-effect filtration and sand-bio-char stacks, we convert rooftop runoff and atmospheric moisture into potable, living water.

The Protocol

  1. Intercept the Stream: Retrofit existing downspouts with "First-Flush" diverters. This mechanical lung rejects the first 20L of dirty wash-off before collecting the clean flow.
  2. The Coanda Screen: Install a static, curved mesh screen (1mm gap). Physics forces water to adhere to the curve and fall into the tank, while leaves and debris skid off. Self-cleaning by design.
  3. The Biological Lung: Route water into a "Schmutzdecke" (Slow Sand Filter). A living biofilm on top of the sand eats pathogens, purifying water to 99.9% bacterial reduction without chemicals.
  4. Storage & Pressure: Gravity-feed into opaque, biomass-clad IBC totes. Elevate 1m for passive pressure.

Why It Matters

Water sovereignty is the ultimate security. A decentralized catchment system doesn't just provide hydration; it reduces urban flooding, lowers municipal energy costs, and reconnects the community to the rhythm of the weather. We drink when it rains.
